# Launching Field Notes: Redrawing Lines and Connecting Spaces
**Date:** June 4, 2026

Welcome to my personal research blog, **"Field Notes."** As a researcher navigating the fields of sociology, international public policy, and legal studies, I often find that the most profound insights don't always fit neatly into a structured academic journal or a formal conference presentation. They happen in the margins—during a walk through a new city, in the middle of an immersive fieldwork experience, or while observing the fluid boundaries of online subcultures.

This space is dedicated to capturing those dynamic, raw fragments of thought.

### What to Expect Here

Under the umbrella of **Actor-Network Theory (ANT)**, I view the world not as a static structure, but as a continuous web of connections. In this blog, I plan to share short essays, conceptual puzzles, and field observations centering around three main pillars:

1. **Queer Trajectories:** Exploring how queer lives, bodies, and identities move through, negotiate, and sometimes resist rigid normative and legal frameworks.
2. **Fandom & Alter Egos:** Investigating communities—such as the furry fandom—and parsing how concepts like the *fursona* act as active mediators that reshape our relationships.
3. **Reflections on Movement:** Notes from my academic travels, project collaborations, and the everyday experience of bridging different cultural contexts.

### Keeping the Dialogue Open

Academic inquiry shouldn't be a one-way lecture; it is a conversation. I wanted this portfolio to feel less like a static CV and more like an open, inviting space. 

Whether you are a fellow researcher, a member of the communities I study, or simply someone who stumbled upon this site out of curiosity, I invite you to read along. If anything here resonates with you, please feel free to drop a message through the **Contact** page or my SNS. 

Thank you for stepping into my network. Let’s see where these lines lead us.
